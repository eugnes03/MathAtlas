Corollary
Category Theory · category.tex
Let $\mathcal{C}$ be a category (Definition~Category). The relation $$ X \cong Y $$ defined via isomorphisms (Definition~Isomorphism) is an equivalence relation on $\operatorname{Ob}(\mathcal{C})$.
Proof
Reflexivity. For every object $X$, the identity morphism $\operatorname{id}_X$ from Definition~Category satisfies $$ \operatorname{id}_X \circ \operatorname{id}_X = \operatorname{id}_X. $$ Thus $\operatorname{id}_X$ is an isomorphism. Symmetry. If $f : X \to Y$ is an isomorphism, Definition~Isomorphism provides an inverse $f^{-1} : Y \to X$. Transitivity. If $f : X \to Y$ and $g : Y \to Z$ are isomorphisms, then $$ (g \circ f)^{-1} = f^{-1} \circ g^{-1} $$ by associativity of composition in Definition~Category.
