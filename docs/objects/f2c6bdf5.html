Definition

Category

Category Theory · category.tex
A category $\mathcal{C}$ consists of the following data:
  1. A collection of objects, denoted $\operatorname{Ob}(\mathcal{C})$.
  2. For every pair of objects $X,Y \in \operatorname{Ob}(\mathcal{C})$, a set $$ \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(X,Y) $$ whose elements are called morphisms (or arrows) from $X$ to $Y$.
  3. For every triple of objects $X,Y,Z$, a composition map $$ \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(Y,Z) \times \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(X,Y) \to \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(X,Z), $$ written $$ (g,f) \mapsto g \circ f . $$
  4. For every object $X$, an identity morphism $$ \operatorname{id}_X \in \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(X,X). $$
These data satisfy the following axioms:
  1. Associativity: For morphisms $$ X \xrightarrow{f} Y \xrightarrow{g} Z \xrightarrow{h} W $$ we have $$ h \circ (g \circ f) = (h \circ g) \circ f . $$
  2. Identity: For every morphism $f : X \to Y$, $$ f \circ \operatorname{id}_X = f \qquadand\qquad \operatorname{id}_Y \circ f = f . $$
